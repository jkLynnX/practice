# TurboTax Step-by-Step Walkthrough - 2025

This guide walks you through every section of TurboTax mapped to your specific situation. Have all your documents from the Master Checklist ready before starting.

> **Recommended TurboTax Version:** TurboTax Self-Employed or TurboTax Premium — you need Schedule F support for the farm.

---

## Before You Start

- [ ] Download your 2024 return from TurboTax (it can auto-import prior year data)
- [ ] Have all documents from `01-MASTER-CHECKLIST.md` gathered
- [ ] Complete `05-HOME-OFFICE-WORKSHEET.md` with your numbers
- [ ] Complete `06-SCHEDULE-F-FARM-WORKSHEET.md` with your numbers
- [ ] Have your bank account and routing number ready (for refund direct deposit)

## Section 2: Income

### Step 2A: W-2 Wages

1. TurboTax will prompt: **"Did you work for an employer?"** → Yes
2. Enter your W-2 information:
   - You can **import** it by entering your employer's EIN, or
   - **Manually enter** each box from your W-2
3. Key boxes: Box 1 (wages), Box 2 (federal tax withheld), Box 12 codes
4. If you have multiple W-2s, add each one

### Step 2B: Interest & Dividends

1. **"Did you earn interest income?"** → Enter 1099-INT amounts (from banks)
2. **"Did you receive dividends?"** → Enter 1099-DIV amounts (if applicable)

### Step 2C: Farm Income (Schedule F) — THIS IS KEY

1. TurboTax will ask about **self-employment/business income**
2. Look for: **"Do you have farm income?"** or navigate to **Self-Employment** > **Farm Income**
3. If TurboTax asks what type of business:
   - Select **"Farm"** or **"Farming"**
   - Business name: **LynnX Tree Farm** (or your farm name)
   - EIN: Enter if you have one, or use your SSN
   - Business code: **111421** (Nursery and Tree Production) or **113110** (Timber Tract Operations)
   - Accounting method: **Cash**
   - Did you materially participate? **Yes** (you actively manage the farm)

4. **Farm Income:**
   - If you had no sales: Enter **$0** for all income categories
   - If you received any agricultural program payments: enter those

5. **Farm Expenses** (enter each from your `06-SCHEDULE-F-FARM-WORKSHEET.md`):

   TurboTax will walk through each category. Enter your totals:

   | TurboTax Prompt | What to Enter |
   |---|---|
   | Car and truck expenses | Total farm mileage x 65.5 cents, OR actual vehicle expenses |
   | Chemicals | Herbicides, pesticides purchased |
   | Fertilizers and lime | All fertilizer for farm |
   | Labor hired | Contractor/worker payments |
   | Rent or lease - equipment | Equipment rentals |
   | Repairs and maintenance | Farm equipment/property repairs |
   | Seeds and plants | Tree seedlings, seeds purchased |
   | Supplies | Tools, gloves, irrigation supplies, misc |
   | Taxes | Property tax on FARM LAND only |
   | Utilities | Electric/water for farm (if separate from home) |
   | Other expenses | Anything else farm-related |

6. **Equipment & Property (Depreciation/Section 179):**
   - TurboTax will ask: **"Did you buy any assets or equipment for your farm?"**
   - Enter each major purchase (fencing, storage buildings, equipment, tools over ~$200)
   - For each item, TurboTax will ask:
     - Description
     - Date purchased
     - Cost
     - Business use percentage (100% if purely for farm)
   - **Choose Section 179** to expense the full cost in 2025 (TurboTax will guide you)

## Section 3: Home Office (Form 8829)

TurboTax will prompt this **within the farm/business section**, not separately.

1. **"Did you use part of your home for this business?"** → **Yes**
2. **"Was it used exclusively for business?"** → **Yes**
3. **Method selection:**
   - **Regular method** (recommended — usually gives a higher deduction)
   - Simplified method ($5/sq ft, max $1,500)
4. If Regular method, enter:
   - Total square footage of your home: _____
   - Square footage of office: _____
   - TurboTax calculates the business percentage
5. Enter home expenses:
   - Mortgage interest (from Form 1098)
   - Property tax on home
   - Electricity (annual total)
   - Gas/heating (annual total)
   - Water (annual total)
   - Internet (annual total)
   - Homeowners insurance (annual premium)
   - Home repairs/maintenance (annual total)
6. TurboTax will calculate the deduction and add it to your Schedule F expenses

> **Tip:** Compare the Regular method result with $5 x office sq ft. Take the higher one.

## Section 4: Deductions

### Step 4A: TurboTax Will Compare Standard vs. Itemized

TurboTax automatically calculates both and recommends the higher one. But make sure you enter everything:

### Step 4B: Itemized Deduction Entries (Schedule A)

| TurboTax Prompt | What to Enter | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mortgage interest | From 1098, Box 1 **minus** the portion already claimed on home office | TurboTax may handle this split automatically |
| Property taxes (home) | From tax statement **minus** the portion claimed on home office | Subject to $10,000 SALT cap |
| Property taxes (farm) | **Do NOT enter here** — this goes on Schedule F | Already entered in farm expenses |
| Charitable donations - cash | Total cash donations to qualified charities | Keep receipts for donations over $250 |
| Charitable donations - goods | Fair market value of donated items (Goodwill, etc.) | Get and keep donation receipts |

### Step 4C: Above-the-Line Deductions

TurboTax will also ask about these (they reduce income regardless of standard/itemized):

- [ ] Student loan interest (up to $2,500)
- [ ] Traditional IRA contributions (up to $7,000 / $8,000 if 50+)
- [ ] HSA contributions (if applicable)
- [ ] Educator expenses (if applicable, up to $300)

## Section 5: Credits

TurboTax will check for applicable credits. Review these:

| Credit | Applies? | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Earned Income Credit | Maybe | Depends on income level (farm loss reduces AGI, may help qualify) |
| Saver's Credit | Maybe | If you contributed to retirement accounts and income is moderate |
| Energy Credits | No | You didn't do energy efficiency upgrades (confirm) |
| Child Tax Credit | No | Single, no dependents (confirm) |
| Education Credits | Maybe | If you took classes or paid tuition |

## Section 6: Review & Optimize

### Key Things to Verify Before Filing

1. **Schedule F shows a loss** — Check that your farm expenses are greater than farm income ($0), resulting in a net loss
2. **Farm loss is reducing your taxable income** — Look at your 1040 line 8 (total income). It should be W-2 wages MINUS farm loss
3. **Home office deduction is included** — Check Form 8829 is attached and the amount flows to Schedule F
4. **Standard vs. Itemized** — TurboTax should show which is higher. Verify the recommendation makes sense
5. **No red flags** — TurboTax will flag potential issues. Address any warnings

### Where to See Your Refund

- TurboTax shows a running refund/owed amount at the top or bottom of the screen
- After entering Schedule F with your farm loss, you should see the refund increase significantly

---

## Section 7: File

1. **Review the summary** — TurboTax shows all forms being filed
2. **Expected forms:**
   - Form 1040
   - Schedule F (Farm Income)
   - Form 8829 (Home Office) — attached to Schedule F
   - Schedule A (only if itemizing)
   - Schedule SE (only if farm shows profit — likely N/A this year)
3. **State return:** None needed — Texas has no state income tax
4. **Payment/refund:**
   - Enter bank account and routing number for **direct deposit**
   - Review the refund amount
5. **E-file:** Submit electronically
6. **Keep a copy:** Download the completed return PDF and save to `docs/other/`

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|---------|
| Can't find farm/Schedule F option | Make sure you have TurboTax Self-Employed or Premium edition. The free/basic versions don't support Schedule F |
| TurboTax says farm is a hobby | Enter information showing profit motive (business plan, active management). If it still flags, override or consult a CPA |
| Home office not showing up | Make sure you enter it within the farm business section, not as a standalone deduction |
| Farm loss not reducing income | Check that you answered "Yes" to material participation. Passive farm losses have different rules |
| 1099-NEC for contractors | If you need to file these, TurboTax has a separate section. Do this before filing your own return if possible |
