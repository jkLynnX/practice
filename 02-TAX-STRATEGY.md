### Section 179 Expensing (Immediate Write-Off)

For equipment and farm property improvements purchased in 2025, you may be able to deduct the **full cost in year one** instead of depreciating over several years:

- **Section 179 limit for 2025:** Up to $1,250,000 (more than enough for most farm purchases)
- **Eligible items:** Equipment, tools, fencing, storage buildings, certain land improvements
- **How to claim:** TurboTax will ask about Section 179 when you enter assets/equipment

### Hobby Loss Rules - CRITICAL

The IRS may reclassify your farm as a "hobby" if it doesn't show profit. Farming gets a favorable rule:

- **Farms:** Must show profit in **2 out of 7 years** (not the usual 3 out of 5)
- **Your situation:** Trees are actively growing — this supports profit motive
- **Protect yourself by documenting:**
  - [ ] Written business plan for the tree farm
  - [ ] Records of what species you're growing and expected harvest timeline
  - [ ] Evidence of active management (receipts, contractor work, your time spent)
  - [ ] Market research or contacts for future tree sales
  - [ ] Texas ag exemption approval (shows the state recognizes this as farming)

> **Important:** If the IRS audits and determines this is a hobby, ALL farm loss deductions would be disallowed. Keep thorough records.

## B. Home Office Deduction (Form 8829)

### You Qualify

You have a **dedicated room used exclusively and regularly** for managing the tree farm business. This qualifies for the home office deduction using the **Regular Method** (actual expenses).

### How It Works

Your home office deduction is a **percentage of your total home expenses**, based on the square footage of your office relative to your entire home.

**Business Percentage = Office Square Feet / Total Home Square Feet**

Example: 150 sq ft office / 2,000 sq ft home = **7.5%**

## D. Personal Home Improvements — The Honest Truth

### What's NOT Deductible

Personal home improvements (kitchen remodel, new flooring, bathroom update, paint, etc.) are **NOT tax deductible** for your primary residence. This is a common misconception.

### What IS Valuable

These improvements **increase your home's cost basis**. When you eventually sell your home, you can:
- Exclude up to **$250,000** in capital gains (single filer)
- Your cost basis = Purchase price + improvements
- Higher basis = less taxable gain if you exceed the exclusion

### Action Item

- Keep all receipts organized in `docs/home-improvements/`
- Maintain a running total of improvement costs
- This will matter when you sell the home, even if it's years from now

### Exceptions — What IS Deductible

- **Home office improvements:** If you improved the office room specifically (new desk built-in, dedicated lighting, etc.), those are 100% deductible on Schedule F
- **Farm property improvements:** Fencing, barns, sheds, irrigation on farm land are deductible/depreciable on Schedule F
- **Energy efficiency credits:** Solar panels, heat pumps, insulation, energy-efficient windows qualify for federal tax credits (you indicated you didn't do these, but check in case)

## E. LynnX LLC — Recommendations

### Current Status: Dormant

### Option 1: Activate for the Tree Farm (Recommended)

**Benefits:**
- Liability protection for farming activities
- Looks more legitimate as a business to the IRS (helps with hobby loss defense)
- Single-member LLC = "disregarded entity" for federal taxes — still files on Schedule F, no separate tax return needed
- Can open a business bank account to separate farm finances

**Steps to activate:**
1. File any overdue Texas franchise tax reports (No Tax Due) at the Texas Comptroller website
2. Get an EIN from the IRS (free, online at irs.gov) if you don't already have one
3. Open a business checking account
4. Start running farm transactions through the LLC

**Tax impact:** Zero additional complexity — single-member LLC income/expenses still go on your personal Schedule F

### Option 2: Keep Dormant

- Still must file annual Texas franchise tax "No Tax Due" report
- No federal tax implications
- Missing out on liability protection and business legitimacy

### Option 3: Use for a Different Venture

- Would file Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business) separately from the farm
- Only makes sense if you have a specific business idea
- Could be used for consulting, freelancing, or other income-generating activity

### Texas Franchise Tax

- Required annually regardless of activity
- **No Tax Due** if total revenue is below $2.47M (you're well under this)
- File online at the Texas Comptroller's website
- Due date: May 15 each year
