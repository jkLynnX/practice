# Receipt Collection Guide - 2025

Step-by-step instructions for downloading receipts and tax documents from every source.

---

## Home Depot

### Online Account (homedepot.com)

1. Go to **homedepot.com** and log in
2. Click **My Account** (top right)
3. Click **Purchase History** in the left sidebar
4. Set the date range to **January 1, 2025 - December 31, 2025**
5. For each purchase:
   - Click **View Receipt** or **View Order Details**
   - **Print to PDF** (Ctrl+P > Save as PDF) or screenshot
   - Save to the appropriate folder:
     - Farm supplies/equipment → `docs/farm-expenses/`
     - Home office items → `docs/home-office/`
     - Personal home improvements → `docs/home-improvements/`

### Pro Account (if you have one)

1. Log into **homedepot.com/c/Pro**
2. Go to **Pro Dashboard** > **Purchase History**
3. You can **export** purchases as a CSV or PDF
4. This is the easiest way to get a full year of receipts at once

### In-Store Purchases (no account linked)

- Check if you used a **credit/debit card** — pull receipts from your card statements instead
- If you gave your **phone number or email** at checkout, receipts may be in your account
- Physical receipts: gather and scan/photograph them

## Lowe's

### MyLowe's Account (lowes.com)

1. Go to **lowes.com** and log in
2. Click **My Account** > **Purchase History**
3. Filter by **2025**
4. For each purchase:
   - Click on the order to see details
   - **Print to PDF** or screenshot
   - Save to the appropriate `docs/` subfolder

### Lowe's for Pros

1. Log into your Lowe's Pro account
2. Go to **Purchase History** > **Export**
3. Download as CSV for easy categorizing

### Receipt Lookup (if you forgot to log in)

1. Go to **lowes.com/l/help/find-a-receipt**
2. Enter your credit card number, purchase date range, and store
3. Lowe's can look up receipts tied to your payment method

## Tractor Supply / Other Farm Stores

### Tractor Supply (tractorsupply.com)

1. Log in > **My Account** > **Order History**
2. Download/screenshot each receipt
3. Save to `docs/farm-expenses/`

### Other stores (Harbor Freight, local nurseries, etc.)

- Check online accounts for purchase history
- Pull from credit card statements if no account exists
- Gather physical receipts and scan them

## Credit Card Statements

This is your **backup method** for any receipts you can't find online. It also helps you find purchases you forgot about.

### For each credit card:

1. Log into your credit card's website/app
2. Go to **Statements** or **Activity**
3. Download **all 12 monthly statements for 2025** (or the annual summary if available)
4. Alternatively, download **transaction history as CSV**:
   - Set date range: 01/01/2025 - 12/31/2025
   - Export as CSV or Excel
5. Search/filter for these merchant names:
   - `HOME DEPOT`
   - `LOWES`
   - `TRACTOR SUPPLY`
   - `HARBOR FREIGHT`
   - Any nursery or farm supply store names
   - Contractor names
   - Gas stations (for farm fuel)

### Categorize Transactions

For each relevant purchase, label it as:
- **FARM** — farm supplies, equipment, seeds, fertilizer, contractor payments
- **HOME OFFICE** — office furniture, supplies, equipment for the office room
- **HOME IMPROVEMENT** — personal home updates (not deductible, but track for basis)

## Bank Statements

1. Log into your bank's website
2. Download **2025 statements** (all 12 months)
3. Look for:
   - Contractor payments (checks written for farm work)
   - Large payments to supply stores
   - Property tax payments
   - Mortgage payments
4. Cross-reference with credit card statements to make sure nothing is missed

---

## Mortgage Company (Form 1098)

1. Log into your mortgage servicer's website
2. Go to **Tax Documents** or **Year-End Statements** or **1098**
3. Download **Form 1098 - Mortgage Interest Statement** for 2025
4. This shows:
   - Box 1: Mortgage interest paid
   - Box 3: Mortgage origination date
   - Box 10: Property taxes paid from escrow
5. Save to `docs/1098/`

> **Note:** If your property tax is paid through escrow, Box 10 of the 1098 shows the amount. If you pay property tax directly, get the statement from the county.

---

## County Tax Assessor (Property Taxes)

### For your PRIMARY HOME:

1. Go to your county's tax assessor/collector website
2. Search by your name or property address
3. Download or screenshot the **2025 tax statement** showing amount paid
4. Save to `docs/property-tax/`

### For your FARM LAND:

1. Same website — search for the farm property
2. Download the **2025 tax statement**
3. Also download any **ag exemption** documentation showing the reduced valuation
4. Save to `docs/property-tax/`

> **Tip:** If home and farm are in different counties, check both county websites.

---

## Employer (W-2)

1. Log into your company's **payroll portal** (ADP, Paychex, Workday, etc.)
2. Go to **Tax Documents** or **Year-End Forms**
3. Download **W-2 for 2025**
4. Save to `docs/w2/`

> Most employers make W-2s available by **January 31**. If you don't see it, contact HR.

---

## Contractor Documentation

For each contractor you hired for farm work:

1. Gather their **invoices** (check email, texts, physical files)
2. Record:
   - Contractor name
   - Amount paid in 2025
   - What work was done
   - Date(s) of service
3. **If you paid any contractor $600 or more in 2025**, you are required to file a **Form 1099-NEC** for them
   - You'll need their name, address, SSN or EIN
   - File by January 31 of the following year (may already be past due — file ASAP if needed)
   - TurboTax can help you file 1099-NEC forms

---

## TurboTax Prior Year Return

1. Log into **turbotax.intuit.com**
2. Go to **Tax Home** > **Prior Year Returns**
3. Download your **2024 tax return PDF**
4. This is useful for:
   - Reference for what you filed last year
   - Carry-forward amounts
   - Your AGI (needed to e-file)
5. Save to `docs/other/`

---

## Health Insurance (1095 Form)

1. Check your **employer portal** for Form 1095-C (employer-provided insurance)
2. Or check **healthcare.gov** for Form 1095-A (marketplace insurance)
3. Or check your **insurer** for Form 1095-B
4. Save to `docs/other/`
